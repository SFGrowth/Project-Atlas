# Atlas Research Specification: Sprint 026 — Execution Model Characterisation

## 1. Context and Objective

**Research Stream:** B — Execution Intelligence  
**Sprint:** 026  
**Status:** Planned

In Sprint 025, Atlas Execution Model A1 earned its promotion to a trusted model by surviving independent stress testing. A critical discovery during validation was that the model's edge is overwhelmingly concentrated in the PM session. 

Following the newly established **Characterisation Principle**, the objective of Sprint 026 is to fully map the operational behaviour of Model A1. No parameters will be optimised. The goal is observation and characterisation, producing the permanent specification document that Stream C (Capital Intelligence) will use to design the Guardian Risk Engine.

## 2. The Frozen Configuration: Model A1

- **Structural Condition:** Volatility Expansion (`ATR(5) / ATR(5)[20] > 1.8`)
- **Trend Alignment:** EMA9/21/50 stack alignment
- **Trigger:** Touch/cross of EMA21
- **Depth Constraint:** Distance from recent 10-bar swing extreme to current close must be between `0.5 and 1.2 * ATR(14)`
- **Structure:** 1-leg pullback
- **Timeframe:** 5-minute MNQ
- **Risk/Reward:** 1:2 (Stop = 1.0 ATR, Target = 2.0 ATR)

## 3. Characterisation Dimensions

The characterisation harness will analyse the 286 trades generated by Model A1 across the following dimensions:

**1. Temporal Decomposition**
- **PM Session by Hour:** 12:00-13:00, 13:00-14:00, 14:00-15:00, 15:00-16:00.
- **Day of Week:** Monday through Friday performance distribution.
- **Monthly Consistency:** Win rate and Net P&L per calendar month.

**2. Structural Decomposition**
- **Long vs Short:** Re-verifying symmetry with deeper metrics (duration, streak probability).
- **Volatility Quartiles:** Performance mapped against the broader daily ATR (e.g., does it perform better in high VIX or low VIX environments?).
- **Trend Strength Quartiles:** Performance mapped against ADX(14) at the time of entry.

**3. Operational Behaviour**
- **Trade Duration Distribution:** Average time in trade for winners vs losers.
- **Consecutive Outcomes:** Distribution of consecutive winners and losers.
- **Losing Streak Probability:** The mathematical probability of hitting N consecutive losses.
- **Risk-of-Ruin Estimation:** Using the Kelly Criterion and empirical drawdowns to estimate the maximum safe risk per trade for a $50k prop firm account.
- **Equity Curve Stability:** R-squared of the equity curve to measure linearity.

## 4. Deliverables

This sprint will produce:
1. `atlas_sprint_026_characterisation.py` — The analysis harness.
2. `atlas-sprint-026-model-a1-characterisation-results.md` — The raw research findings.
3. `atlas-model-a1-specification.md` — The permanent, formal operational specification for Model A1, detailing exactly when it should be traded and when Guardian should block it.
